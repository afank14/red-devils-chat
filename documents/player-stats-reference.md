# Manchester United: complete statistical reference

**Wayne Rooney leads Manchester United's all-time scoring chart with 253 goals in 559 appearances, while Ryan Giggs holds the club appearance record at 963 games across 23 seasons.** This reference compiles verified statistics across six key categories — all-time scorers, appearance records, decade-by-decade leaders, single-season records, European competition stats, and Premier League era figures. Numbers are drawn from official Manchester United records (manutd.com), UEFA.com, premierleague.com, Wikipedia's club records page, and specialist databases including mufcinfo.com and Transfermarkt. Where sources conflict, discrepancies are flagged and the most authoritative figure is used.

## Source Notes

### All-time scorers discrepancies

- **Van Nistelrooy appearances** are listed as 219 by most authoritative sources (Wikipedia, Goal.com, manutd.com reference to total appearances); 90min.com lists 210, which likely counts starts only. **Use 219.**
- **Cristiano Ronaldo appearances** are cited as 346 (Sky Sports, MessivsRonaldo.app) and 345 (Goal.com). The 346 figure is corroborated by adding first spell (~292) and second spell (~54). **Use 346.**
- **George Best appearances** are given as 470 by the official Manchester United site and most sources, but Wikipedia's Best article cites 474 appearances and 181 goals. The difference likely stems from counting Charity Shield or other minor matches. **The official club figure is 470/179.**
- **Rashford** is currently on loan at FC Barcelona (2025–26 season). His Man Utd totals of 426 apps and 138 goals are per the official club site as of his departure on loan.
- **Denis Irwin**, requested for inclusion, scored only **33 goals in 529 appearances** (1990–2002). He was a full-back and does not rank among the top scorers; he appears instead in the appearances table.

### Appearance count discrepancies

- **Giggs 963 vs. 967**: The MUFC Fandom Wiki lists 967; this likely includes Community Shield appearances. The official manutd.com and Wikipedia both use **963 competitive appearances**.
- **Rooney 559 vs. 592**: The Fandom Wiki's 592 likely includes Community Shield, Club World Cup, and other non-competitive matches. The official figure is **559**.
- **Stepney 535 vs. 539**: Minor 4-appearance gap likely reflects Charity Shield counting. Official figure: **535**.
- **Bryan Robson 461 vs. 470**: Wikipedia and the National Football Museum agree on 461; the higher figure from Fandom Wiki includes minor competitions. **Use 461.**
- **Albiston goals**: Official manutd.com says 7; Wikipedia says 6. Most sources agree on **7**.
- **John Aston Sr.**: 284 apps / 30 goals (Wikipedia) vs. 272 / 29 (alternative databases). Pre-1950s records carry inherent uncertainty. Aston's career was cut short by tuberculosis.

### Decade estimate methodology

Decade goal totals are estimates derived from career timelines and total career figures. Precise season-by-season decade breakdowns require a granular match-by-match database. Van Nistelrooy's 150 is exact (entire career at United fell within the 2000s). Current-player figures (Fernandes, Rashford, Garnacho) are approximate as the 2025–26 season is still in progress.

### UEFA competition counting

UEFA's official tally includes qualifying-round goals (Manchester United entered CL qualifying in 2005–06), adding approximately 3 goals to affected players' totals. The "main comp." column from free-elements.com excludes these qualifying matches. For pre-Champions League European Cup campaigns (1956–1968), only the main competition existed, so there is no discrepancy for Law, Charlton, or Best. Only the top 6 appearance figures (Giggs through Solskjær) are confirmed directly from UEFA.com; ranks 7–10 are strong estimates based on career spans and participation records.

### Source reliability summary

The most critical pattern across all six categories is that **fan-maintained wikis consistently inflate appearance figures** by 3–8% compared to official club records, largely by including Community Shield, Club World Cup, and other non-competitive matches in their totals. The official manutd.com, premierleague.com, and UEFA.com figures should be treated as authoritative. CL appearances beyond the top 6 are strong estimates derived from career timelines but require Transfermarkt verification for exactness. Decade-by-decade goal totals are largely estimates, as no single authoritative source publishes exact decade breakdowns for all players.

### Cross-referencing recommendation

For maximum accuracy in a published reference, cross-checking individual player statistics on **Transfermarkt** (which provides season-by-season breakdowns by competition) against the official club records on **manutd.com** is recommended for any figure where precision to the single digit matters.
